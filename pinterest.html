    Pinterest Story Pin

    Duration: March 2020-Present

    My Role: Product Designer(contract)

    To help empower people to discover things they love and inspire then to go do those things in real life.

    Image credit: Pinterest Newsroom Website

    Introducing Story Pin!

    People come to Pinterest to be inspired—to turn dreams into next steps to see things in a new way, and to truly be themselves. Story Pins provide Pinners with the content they most want, and will incentivize creators to make new content for Pinterest by offering them an exciting new way to engage with audiences on Pinterest.

    Image credit: Pinterest on YouTube

    My Role

    As a product designer, I contributed to various flows for Story Pins in beta (Creation, User Education, Sharing and Multi Drafts, Visual Response, Product tagging, Creator Attribution, etc.) by executing ideas, research, wireframe, prototypes, documentation, etc. For each project, I worked along with 1 product manager, 1 design lead, 1 writer, engineers(iOS/Android/UXE), and researchers.

    The Challenge

    Our ultimate goal was to provide Pins that are relevant, unique, and actionable. We call this ideal content “native content” and it’s supplied almost entirely by content creators. To achieve our goals of 50% native content in 3 years, we needed to build a content creation tool that inspires both Pinners and creators to come to Pinterest more often, and engage more deeply.

    Selected Projects

    Multi Drafts

    Multi Drafts give creators more ways to manage their workflows and schedule their Story Pin content, and it allows additional collaboration opportunities when working with third parties like brands. This was a highly requested feature, especially for more professional creators that are working on multiple projects at a time. Multiple drafts allows users to come back to complete an unfinished project or edit and save multiple Story Pins in advance of publishing.

    User Education

    User education for Story Pin is not merely about teaching users step-by-step instructions, but it is an opportunity for us to introduce this new product and set the proper expectation as our users begin exploring. I worked very closely with a product writer to design and explore effective education for Story Pin users from flow audits to ideation to design handoff.

    Creator Attribution

    Currently, most pinners are not aware and cannot name creators on the platform. We needed to shift pinners so that they start understanding that creators are an important part of the content they discover. Creator attribution, therefore, gives creators credit and makes them feel special on the platform. For September launch, we wanted to provide proper recognition to creators so they feel motivated to build a following to monetize. Furthermore, we saw this as an opportunity to clean up old code that controls attribution and then build a consistent and impactful set of creator attribution across.
