    Onduo Mobile App

    Duration: 2018-2019

    My Role: UI/UX Designer

    A program to help people manage their Type 2 diabetes

    notification_important
    To comply with my non-disclosure agreement, I have omitted and obfuscated confidential information in this case study. All information in this case study is my own and does not necessarily reflect the views of Verily.

    Background

    Onduo, a joint venture between Verily and Sanofi, is a virtual diabetes clinic. Through the app, patients have access to lessons and activities that were designed to understand the impact of food and exercise on blood glucose. This activity provides sufficient information to alert both the patient and their care team in the app if their diabetes control is declining, or to reinforce good behavior if they’re maintaining good glycemic control.

    More importantly, the defined UI & UX patterns for this Paired Check Challenge(PCC) would lay important groundwork by becoming a template for future Checkpoint Challenges. As we expanded the library of self-guided activities, I was in charge of redesigning the Pair Check Activity experience.

    What if managing blood sugar levels became simple and insightful?

    Introducing Paired Check Challenge

    Healthy eating is a cornerstone of healthy living — with or without diabetes. But if you have diabetes, you need to know how foods affect your blood sugar levels. It's not only the type of food you eat but also how much you eat and the combinations of food types you eat.

    Learn from the Previous Version(V1)

    Based off of the previously conducted diary study, I approached this problem from understanding success and failures. This research was a major breakthrough for me and allowed me to focus the energy on creating the right experience, this time. Key takeaways that we found were:

    1. Users' schedules are the root cause of people having difficulty.
    2. Functionality does not accommodate user schedules resulting in usability issues.
    3. Value drivers are not emphasized.

    Customer Journey Map

    I moved forward by focusing on the strengths of the approach to distinguish between where we are now and where we wish to be.

    After analyzing the strengths, it seemed logical to use the existing Framework we designed for other behavior modification and skill building exercises. If you see the framework below, we set proper expectations before asking people to try it out, and then they can schedule it at their convenient time. At the end of the activity, we provide them with useful insights that our members can take aways with. Moving forward, I continued to retain that the “Give-and-Take” rule.

    Redesign the Scheduling Experience

    The app originally provides minimal amount of information what's included in the activity. However, using the existing framework, I separate the single screens into 2 separate screens so that people can focus on the contemt and expectations prior to schedule Paire Check Challenge. Although I explored more on other iterations for 'Schedule', we decided to reuse the Challenge component for this time. However, all my iterations still affected later improvements.

    Redesign the In-Progress Card

    I initially wanted to provide a similar structure with the dynamic 'Log' button, and then ultimately decided to create a version that enables users to both 'Quit' and 'Reschedule' the Challenge. Lastly, I created a iteration that provides an easy option to 'Snooze' the Challenge instead of 'Quit'. In addition, our users reacted well to the large, inviting 'Log' button which only beecomes tappable when the time comes up. I also explored multiple other iterations with small to large tweaks, and they all affected to the fulture iterations.

    Unhappy Path: the Follow-Up Question

    The experience had to encompass various scenarios. We ask questions if our users fail to log food or take readings to collect useful data for improvement. This was a good learning experience for me to come up with an efficient flow for every case.

    Impact

    From the last usability test, all 7 participants could successfully finished Paired Check Challenge and shared positive feedback. Most participants, even those who have never completed challenges before, expressed interest in trying the paired check.

    Next Steps

    Custom Care Pack

    Per our users feedback, we found that people want to learn more about healthy foods and receipes. I took a stab at the early explorations of the healthy package combining with other existing Onduo Lessons and Activities.

    Lesson Learned

    More context the better

    Never assume they will know what they see without explanation. Providing proper context upfront is crucial for our users, and there are various ways to introduce that information depending on the case.


    Setting the right tone is crucial

    UX writing bridges a gap between the visual solutions and the usability, between the product and its users. It isn't just about how to write, but it's also about how we set the right tone.
