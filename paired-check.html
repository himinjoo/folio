    Onduo Mobile App

    Duration: 2018-2019

    My Role: UI/UX Designer

    A program to help people manage their Type 2 diabetes

    Background

    Onduo, a joint venture between Verily and Sanofi, is a virtual diabetes clinic. The mobile app supports its members with resources and tools to help them manage their disease. In order to do so, health coaches would sort through hundreds of google docs to find resources. They would then have to copy that content & send to their members via a messaging window. We needed to create a way to automate and deploy these strategies without coach intervention.

    The Team

    7 designers, 2 product managers, 4 engineers, 1 behavioral scientists, 2 UX researchers, 1UX writer

    My Role as UI/UX Designer

    Collaborating with cross-functional teams, I contributed to the launch of the first Challenge framework by executing wireframes, prototypes, design system, component library, modular illustration system, etc.

    Up until now, I support the team to evolve the features and address customer pain-points related to behavior modification and skill building exercises.


    • Rapidly producing multiple concepts and prototypes; knowing when to apply pixel-perfect attention to detail, and when to make low-fi sketches and prototypes
    • Partnering closely with engineering and product teams to find elegant but practical solutions to design challenges
    • Listening to users to develop the right product strategy and solutions that delight our customers
    • Conceptualize original ideas that bring simplicity and user friendliness to complex UX roadblocks

    The Challenge

    Our challenges were to support Onduo health coach partners to save and use their time more efficiently, and to build more scalable and personalized resources and tools for our users.

    The Solution: Self-Guided Activities

    Automate the coach deployed strategies in order to save the coach’s time so that they can focus more on personalizing care by helping individuals find the right set of strategies that work for their lives.

    In the future world, the product automates the personalization process to help people experiment with different diabetes management tools to find what works for them and incorporate them into their lives.

    500+ Pages of New Strategies

    Collaborating with a behavioral scientist and clinicians, we were designing over 500 pages of stragegies lessons and activities, so called Onduo Challenge. First, we had to create means to organize the behavior change approach, which were broken down into 4 pillar:

    Eat Well

    Eating Healthy

    Be Active

    Encouraging Movement & Exercise

    Take Care

    Doctor Visits & Taking Medication As Prescribed

    Ease Stress

    Healthy Coping of Life's Stresses


    This already starts to organize all the pieces that come with Diabetes Management into an understandable structure. The team combed through those 500+ pages of content & strategies the coaches were pulling from to create program maps for each pillar. Within each map, we identified strategies that could be delivered as lessons (read and learn) and those that could be delivered as activities (try something out).

    The biggest challenge that I

    Testing & Feedback

    The biggest challenge that I faced for throughout the project was balancing designs with feedback, while collaborating with broader team. Since this new system would touch every part of our product, I needed to coordinate and get buy-in from the entire team. This was hard.

    Managing feedback was even more challenging because everyone has different viewpoints. We spent a disproportionate amount of time debating design decisions, when there wasn’t data that helps drive decision.

    “Managing feedback was even more challenging because everyone has different viewpoints.”

    Another designer and I started documentation to help alleviate the data crutch and better articulate design rationale. Doing this was time consuming, but saved tons of time as the project progressed.

    Impact

    Numerous hours of brainstorming, reviewing, careful planning, sketching, designing, and developing has brought the results desired for Onduo app. The first Challenge (Plate Method Challenge) was successfully launched and followed by multiple other Challenges (e.g. Get Moving Challenge and Breakfast Swap Challenge). We don’t waste our time talking about the right font-size, button types, or use of components anymore, but we use the time to develop the right product strategy and solutions that delight our users.

    Next Steps

    A good start.. and it's still day one

    It's still early days for the feature, yet the results have exceeded our expectations. There still requires customization depending on the type of Challenge. Since the launch, we've developed several new UI & UX patterns for new Challenges. The component library is forever evolving.


    Design system for healthcare

    This new design language has been used across all other Verily products as well. This wasn't expected, but it turned out to be a great asset for the whole organization. I started to question what it is like to design for healthcare products. How might we better design for people with various medical conditions? How can we improve accessibility for all?

    Lesson Learned

    Collaboration is key

    The more eyes on a design, the more it is exposed to varying opinions, experience, and critique.


    Process is essential

    A project that is vast gives you a roadmap to navigate through what can be a foggy route. This is especially helpful when you’re starting out.
